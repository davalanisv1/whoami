Engineering & Adaptation

I genuinely love engineering. Whether it's the Robostangs workshop or coding, the process of taking an idea and actually making it work is what I enjoy most. Moving between Mexico and U.S. was a big shift, but it taught me how to be comfortable in new situations. I think being able to figure things out on the fly is just as important in real life as it is in the workshop.

What I do to reset

While I spent my early high school years playing violin in the Symphony Orchestra, I've decided to move on from music to focus more on my engineering goals. These days, my "downtime" looks a lot more normal:

  • The Dog: If I'm outside, I'm probably hanging out with my dog.
  • Screen Time: I'm a big fan of movies and spend a decent amount of time on YouTube, usually keeping up with new tech or just relaxing.
  • Friends: I value my time with my friends—whether we’re out doing something or just hanging out, that's where I recharge.
"I believe the best engineers are the ones who know how to step away from the project, relax, and come back with a fresh perspective."
